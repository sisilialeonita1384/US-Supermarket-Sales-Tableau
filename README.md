# US-Supermarket-Sales-Tableau

This project explores a US supermarket sales dataset to uncover patterns behind revenue, profit, and product performance. Instead of just looking at total sales, the analysis focuses on identifying where the business is actually making or losing money, and how those patterns vary across locations, products, and time.

What I did?
I worked with ~9.9K rows of sales data and built a set of visualizations in Tableau to analyze:
- sales performance across states and cities
- the relationship between sales and profit
- product categories that drive gains vs losses
- sales trends over time (monthly & weekday patterns)
All insights are compiled into an interactive dashboard for easier exploration.

Key insights
- California, Texas, and New York generate the highest sales
- Texas stands out with significant losses despite high sales volume
- High sales don’t always translate to high profit
- Sales patterns vary depending on time (month & day of the week)

Why it matters?
This analysis highlights the importance of looking beyond revenue. By combining sales and profit insights, businesses can better identify inefficiencies and make more informed decisions.

Tools
Tableau

Output
An interactive dashboard that allows users to explore performance by region, product category, and time—making it easier to spot trends and problem areas.
